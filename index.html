About Me

I played goalkeeper at IU Indianapolis, where I captained the team twice and graduated from the Kelley School of Business. After college, I signed professionally with Michigan Stars FC in NISA, attending combines, working with agents, and living the reality of American professional soccer.

That experience shaped what I want to do next. I saw firsthand how front office decisions ripple through a roster, and I became obsessed with the data behind those decisions. Now I combine my business and finance background with skills in Tableau, Power BI, SQL, and Python to explore questions about player valuation, roster construction, and club strategy. I published a piece on American Soccer Analysis examining how goalkeeper performance translates into real financial and sporting value for MLS clubs.

By day, I work as an audit associate at Forvis Mazars, where I analyze financials and work on audits for complex clients across industries, including engagements involving business combinations, foreign currency translation, and intricate data sets. I am on track to earn my CPA license in June 2026. Outside of work, I run goalkeeper sessions at Sporting Springfield and volunteer with Missouri State University's program, analyzing film, preparing for matches, and staying embedded in the game I love.
